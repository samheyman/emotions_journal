# Daylio Competitor Research

Research compiled: February 2026

## 1. Product Features and Capabilities

### Core Mood Tracking
- **Five-point mood scale**: Users can log their mood on a simple five-point scale with customizable emojis
- **Haptic feedback**: Recent addition provides gentle vibration when logging moods or updating entries, making check-ins more interactive
- **Quick entry design**: Designed for minimal time investment - users can log their day in seconds without lengthy writing

### Activity and Habit Tracking
- **Pre-defined and custom activities**: Users can select from pre-made activity lists or create their own custom activities
- **Full customization**: Complete control over moods, activities, emojis, icons, and color themes
- **Activity correlation analysis**: The app automatically analyzes which activities are most frequently associated with certain moods

### Journaling Capabilities
- **Optional notes**: While designed for quick logging, users can add detailed written entries if desired
- **Writing templates**: Pre-built templates to help structure journal entries
- **Photo attachments**: Up to three photos can be added per entry
- **Voice memos**: Audio recording capability for entries

### New Feature: Scales (Recently Added)
- **Multi-dimensional tracking**: A new way to track various aspects beyond basic mood
- **Customizable sliders**: Track sleep quality, stress levels, energy, pain, and other metrics using simple slider interfaces
- **Granular data**: Provides more nuanced tracking compared to the basic five-point mood scale

### Analytics and Insights
- **Advanced statistics**: Among the most comprehensive stats in mood tracking apps
- **Visual data presentation**: Mood charts displaying trends over days, weeks, or months
- **Correlation analysis**: Shows relationships between activities and mood states
- **Mood lines and trends**: Track patterns over time with visual representations

### Goal Setting and Motivation
- **Daily, weekly, and monthly goals**: Set targets for building positive habits
- **Daily streak tracking**: Monitor consecutive days of logging
- **Achievement system**: Gamification elements to encourage consistent use

### Privacy and Security
- **Local data storage**: All data stored on device, not in cloud by default
- **No data collection**: Daylio does not collect or store user entries on their servers
- **Security options**: PIN lock, fingerprint, or Face ID protection
- **100% private**: Marketed as a completely private journaling experience

### Data Export and Backup
- **Backup file format**: Proprietary .daylio extension containing all entries, moods, activities, and settings
- **CSV export**: Export data in CSV format for analysis in spreadsheet applications
- **PDF export**: Generate PDF reports of entries
- **Manual backup**: Export to email, Google Drive, Dropbox, and other cloud services
- **Cross-platform transfer**: Backup files can be moved between iOS and Android devices

### Integrations
- **Limited native integrations**: No comprehensive API or built-in calendar sync
- **Export-based workflows**: Integration with other apps primarily through CSV exports
- **Third-party imports**: Other journaling apps (like Journey, Day One) can import Daylio CSV files
- **Community requests**: Users have requested health app integrations and calendar sync, but these are not officially available

## 2. Target Market and Positioning

### Primary Target Audience
- **Mental health and wellness enthusiasts**: Individuals interested in tracking and understanding their emotional patterns
- **Casual mood trackers**: People who want simple, quick mood logging without extensive journaling commitment
- **Mental health condition management**: Users with depression, bipolar disorder, anxiety, and other mood-related conditions
- **ADHD community**: Individuals looking to track patterns and build habits (though setup complexity can be a barrier)
- **Self-improvement focused users**: People interested in habit formation and personal data analysis

### Brand Positioning
- **Simplicity-first approach**: Positioned as the easiest, quickest way to track moods
- **"Micro-journaling" concept**: Emphasizes quick check-ins over lengthy journal entries
- **Data-driven self-understanding**: Focus on using personal analytics to identify patterns
- **Privacy-conscious**: Strong emphasis on local storage and zero data collection
- **Customization and power**: Marketed as the most customizable and feature-rich option for advanced users

### Pricing Model (Freemium)

**Free Version:**
- Basic mood tracking on five-point scale
- Activity logging
- Basic statistics and charts
- One daily reminder
- Limited goals
- Core journaling functionality

**Premium Version:**
- **Monthly**: $4.99/month
- **Annual**: $35.99/year (approximately $3.00/month)

**Premium Features:**
- Advanced statistics and analytics
- Unlimited goals
- Multiple daily reminders
- Automatic cloud backups
- PIN lock and security features
- Export options (PDF, CSV)
- Ad-free experience
- Additional customization options

**Value Proposition:**
- Free version is robust enough for most users' basic needs
- Premium pricing is competitive within the mental health app market
- Annual subscription offers significant savings over monthly option

## 4. User Reviews and Sentiment

### Overall Ratings
- **Apple App Store**: 4.8 stars (45,700+ reviews)
- **Google Play Store**: 4.8 stars (393,000+ reviews)

### What Users Love

**Simplicity and Ease of Use:**
- Consistently praised as "quick and easy"
- Users appreciate the ability to log moods even when tired or upset
- Minimal time investment required for daily tracking
- Interface is intuitive and unintimidating

**Effectiveness for Pattern Recognition:**
- Users report genuine insights into emotional patterns
- Activity correlation features help identify mood triggers
- Visual charts make it easy to spot trends over time
- Helps users understand what affects their day-to-day mood

**Customization Options:**
- Described as "the most customizable and feature-rich application for power users"
- Ability to personalize moods, activities, icons, and themes
- Flexibility appeals to users with diverse tracking needs

**Mental Health Utility:**
- "Very helpful in tracking moods if users have any type of mood disorder like depression or bipolar"
- Effective for medication tracking, sleep monitoring, and social activity logging
- Combines ease of use with in-depth personal data analysis

**Free Version Quality:**
- "Most of what users need is available in the free version"
- Premium features feel like nice-to-haves rather than essentials
- Generous free tier makes the app accessible to all users

**Data and Analytics:**
- "Most advanced stats seen in mood tracker apps"
- Superior correlation statistics
- Compelling visual data presentation

### Common Complaints and Dislikes

**Limitations for Severe Mental Health Conditions:**
- Not ideal for users with more severe mental health symptoms
- Lacks specific symptom tracking options for clinical-level conditions
- Some users find the mood options don't capture their specific experiences

**Missing Educational Support:**
- No educational content or guidance for users with mental health issues
- App doesn't provide professional support or crisis resources
- Users are left to interpret their data without expert context

**Reminder Limitations (Free Version):**
- Only one daily reminder available in free version
- Multiple reminders restricted to premium users
- Can be difficult for users who need frequent prompting

**Setup Complexity for ADHD Users:**
- Complete customization requires significant time investment
- Initial setup can be overwhelming for users struggling with activation and motivation
- Paradoxically, those who might benefit most (ADHD users) face the highest setup barrier

**Export Limitations:**
- Photos don't export when creating Excel or PDF reports
- Limited to 3 photos per entry
- Data export features less comprehensive than some users need

**Missing Platform Features:**
- No Apple Watch app (noted in some reviews)
- Limited calendar integration
- No native API for third-party integrations

**Lack of Professional Guidance:**
- App provides data but no interpretation help
- No connection to mental health professionals
- Users with serious conditions may need more than self-tracking

### Sentiment Themes

**Positive Sentiment (Dominant):**
- High satisfaction with core functionality
- Strong appreciation for simplicity and speed
- Users feel empowered by data insights
- Many report long-term consistent use (years)
- Strong word-of-mouth recommendations

**Constructive Criticism:**
- Feature requests rather than complaints
- Desire for expanded capabilities (more photos, better exports)
- Requests for professional features or guidance
- Interest in deeper integrations with health ecosystems

**Overall Assessment:**
- Extremely high user satisfaction (4.8/5.0 across both major platforms)
- Users consistently describe it as best-in-class for mood tracking
- Free version satisfies most users, premium feels fairly priced
- Most criticisms are about missing features rather than poor implementation
- Strong product-market fit for casual to moderate mental health tracking needs
