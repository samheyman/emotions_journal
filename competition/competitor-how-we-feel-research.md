# How We Feel - Competitor Research

*Research conducted: February 2026*

## 1. Product Features and Capabilities

### Core Tracking Mechanism

**Emotion Selection System**
- Users select from 144 nuanced emotion options organized by energy and valence
- Check-ins begin by selecting from four energy-valence quadrants (e.g., high-energy pleasant, low-energy unpleasant)
- Users can choose two specific emotions that best describe their current state
- The emotion tracking system is based on the Mood Meter framework developed by Yale Center for Emotional Intelligence

**Physical Sensations Tracking**
- Added in June 2025 update
- Users can track physical sensations like tight chest or fluttering stomach
- Enhances somatic awareness as users navigate their emotions
- Connects bodily sensations with emotional states

**Check-in Process**
- Users start by checking in with how they're feeling in the moment
- Tag or journal about factors contributing to their current emotional state
- Option to add photos to check-ins
- Can customize sharing preferences for individual check-ins

### Analytics and Insights

**Pattern Recognition**
- Daily HealthKit tracking integration to identify emotional patterns
- Weekly and monthly summaries to monitor emotional patterns over time
- Visual analysis showing emotions over time with color coordination
- Seasonal Snapshot feature to establish baseline for overall well-being

**Data Export**
- Users can export their data for further analysis
- Export includes Reflections and Reflect Takeaways data in CSV format
- Data can be imported on a new device
- Note: Data is stored locally on device, not on servers

### Regulation Strategies

**Video Strategy Library**
- Step-by-step video strategies organized by themes:
  - "Change Your Thinking" (cognitive strategies)
  - "Move Your Body" (movement/physical strategies)
  - "Be Mindful" (mindfulness strategies)
  - "Reach Out" (social strategies)
- Strategies help address negative thought patterns, express emotions, gain perspective, and build trust

**Sound Patterns**
- Audio tool for creating customized soundscapes based on mood
- Provides ambient audio support for emotional regulation

### AI-Powered Features

**Reflect (AI Thought Partner)**
- AI-powered feature designed to make journaling and self-reflection easier and more insightful
- Can ask why users feel a particular way
- Offers suggestions for taking different perspectives
- Helps identify potential thinking traps
- Described by users as a "game changer"
- Recent updates (2025):
  - Over 70 new emotion words added
  - Users can browse Reflect Takeaways as a collection in the Tools Tab
  - Reflect Takeaways included in Weekly Review
  - Full Reflections data included in data export

### Social Features

**Friends Feature**
- Share feelings with trusted people in real time
- Strengthens important relationships through authentic emotional sharing
- Recent enhancements:
  - Commenting on shared check-ins for more thoughtful conversations
  - Default share settings for automatic sharing with selected connections
  - Customizable privacy settings (private, just the feeling, or everything)
  - Can toggle between automatic sharing or manual approval for each check-in
  - Settings can be changed at any time

### Technical Integration

**HealthKit Integration**
- Syncs with Apple HealthKit for comprehensive health tracking
- Correlates sleep, exercise, and health trends with emotional states
- Helps identify patterns between physical health and emotional well-being

## 2. Target Market and Positioning

### Target Audience

**Broad, Inclusive Focus**
The app is designed for multiple use cases and demographics:
- People building better relationships
- Those wanting emotions to work for them, not against them
- Individuals managing stress and anxiety
- Anyone seeking to improve overall emotional well-being

**Therapeutic and Educational Use**
- Therapists recommend the app to their clients for improving communication
- Used in educational settings for emotional intelligence development
- Appeals to users interested in science-based mental health tools

### Brand Positioning

**Scientific Credibility**
- Created in collaboration with Yale University's Center for Emotional Intelligence
- Based on the research of Dr. Marc Brackett, Professor at Yale Child Study Center
- Scientific and content team led by Yale researchers
- Described as a "science-based nonprofit organization"

**Awards and Recognition**
- Chosen as one of the top 15 apps of 2022
- Selected for App Store's "Cultural Impact" Award for design and ability to help people build emotion skills
- 4.9 out of 5-star rating on app stores

**Mission-Driven Positioning**
- Emphasizes accessibility and bringing mental wellbeing to the "widest possible audience"
- Positions emotions as neither good nor bad, focusing on awareness and regulation
- Tagline approach: "Helping Emotions Work for Us, Not Against Us"

### Pricing Model

**Completely Free**
- 100% free with no paywalls or premium tiers
- No advertisements
- Nonprofit model supported by donations
- Relies on donations from individuals passionate about mental wellbeing
- Ensures accessibility for all users regardless of financial means

**Distribution**
- Available on both iOS and Android
- Free download from App Store and Google Play

## 4. User Reviews and Sentiment

### Overall Ratings

**App Store Performance**
- 4.9 out of 5-star rating across platforms
- JustUseApp Legitimacy Score: 65.7/100 (based on 22,812 user reviews)
- Consistently high ratings maintained over time

### What Users Love

**Emotional Intelligence Development**
- Users report improved understanding of their emotions
- App helps users understand what emotions are trying to communicate
- Expands vocabulary for describing emotional states
- Improves communication skills in relationships

**Design and User Experience**
- Haptic feedback, design, and flow described as "extremely impressive"
- Color coordination and visual analysis highly praised
- Intuitive interface and smooth user experience
- Photo feature for adding visual context to check-ins

**Therapeutic Value**
- Therapists actively recommend the app to clients
- Users report improved ability to manage stress and anxiety
- Helps with pattern recognition over time
- Beneficial for understanding emotional triggers

**Accessibility**
- Users appreciate that it's completely free
- No ads or paywalls
- Numerous tools and resources available at no cost
- Mission-driven approach resonates with users

### Common Complaints and Concerns

**AI Privacy Concerns**
- Some users disappointed by addition of AI to the app
- Concerns about private thoughts being used to train AI models
- Worry about data privacy with AI-powered Reflect feature
- Request for more transparency about data usage

**Technical Issues**
- Check-in details page occasionally goes dark during emotion selection
- Users unable to record anything beyond initial emotion when this occurs
- App crashes can result in data loss (since data stored locally)
- Widget not updating correctly (addressed in recent updates)

**Data Management Issues**
- Risk of losing data if device has issues
- Local storage means potential loss of entire year's entries
- Export feature exists but users report it could be more robust
- Lack of cloud backup for redundancy

**User Interface Changes**
- Recent update forced "Reason" entry page to open
- Users previously had option to bypass this step
- Preference for more flexibility in check-in workflow
- Some resistance to UI changes that remove user choice

### Common Themes in Feedback

**Positive Themes**
1. **Life-changing impact**: Users report improved relationships and life quality
2. **Scientific backing**: Appreciation for Yale collaboration and evidence-based approach
3. **Comprehensive features**: Balance of tracking, analysis, and regulation strategies
4. **Authentic mission**: Users trust the nonprofit model and mission-driven approach

**Areas for Improvement**
1. **Data backup**: Strong desire for cloud sync and backup options
2. **AI transparency**: Need for clearer communication about AI data usage
3. **UI flexibility**: Requests for more customizable workflows
4. **Technical stability**: Ongoing need for bug fixes and performance improvements
